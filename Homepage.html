Marine Species

Blue Whale (Balaenoptera Musculus)

The blue whale is the largest animal on Earth, reaching lengths of up to 100 feet. Blue whales are not only remarkable for their size but also for their ecological importance as apex predators in marine ecosystems. They play a crucial role in the balance of ocean food webs and are a symbol of conservation efforts to protect Earth's marine biodiversity. They are found in oceans worldwide and primarily feed on krill by filter-feeding through baleen plates.

Marine Habitats

Coral Reefs

These are underwater structures formed by colonies of tiny coral polyps. Coral reefs are found in tropical and subtropical waters and are known for their high biodiversity, providing habitat for numerous fish, invertebrates, and other marine organisms.
